Information

About

HackNewHaven is the University of New Haven’s student-led hackathon, designed to bring together undergraduate and graduate students (or recent grads) for a weekend of intensive creativity, collaboration, and technical growth. The event is free to attend, beginner-friendly, and features themed challenges, mentorship, and prizes. Students have built web apps, AI tools, games, and more within the hackathon framework.

Teams

All participants must be at least 18 years old and a student of some university (or a recent UNH graduate within less than one year). Teams may be comprised of up to five members. A team may only submit one project, and no participant may be a member of multiple teams. All participants must bring a valid form of identification.

Guidlines

Projects should be original works created on site. Coming with an idea in mind is perfectly fine, working on an existing project is not.

FAQ

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is an invention marathon. Students come together to build cool software & hardware hacks over 24–48 hours. It’s very beginner friendly.

What are the opportunities I can unlock from this event?

This event offers a chance to grow and connect. Collaborate with fellow innovators and mentors, compete for prizes, and build lasting connections.

How much does it cost?

Nothing. Attending a hackathon is free.

Do I need to be a student to attend?

Yes. Only current students or recent graduates (within 12 months) age 18+ may attend.

Where is the event?

The event is hosted at the University of New Haven, West Haven, CT.

What is Major League Hacking?

Major League Hacking is the official student hackathon league in North America & Europe.
